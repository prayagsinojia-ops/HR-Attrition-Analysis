# HR-Attrition-Analysis

## 🧠 1. Project Introduction

Employee attrition is a critical HR challenge that impacts hiring cost, productivity, and organizational stability.

In this project, I performed a complete HR Attrition Analysis using Python (EDA) and Power BI, identifying key drivers behind employee turnover and building a high-risk employee detection system.

This project demonstrates:

- Business understanding

- Data cleaning & EDA skills

- Dashboard development

- Insight extraction

- Strategic HR recommendations


## 🎯 2. Business Objective
- Why are employees leaving?

- Which departments are most affected?

- Does salary, satisfaction, or experience impact attrition?

- Can we identify high-risk employees before they leave?

## 📊 5. Power BI Dashboard Overview

📊 Dashboard 1
 ![Dashboard](https://github.com/prayagsinojia-ops/HR-Attrition-Analysis/blob/main/HR%20D%201.png) 

 Key Insights

- Overall Attrition: 19% (193 employees left).

- 10+ years employees have highest attrition (27%).

- Managers show highest attrition (23%).

- Lower salary band (0–40K) has highest attrition (22%). <br>

 📊 Dashboard 2
 ![Dashboard](https://github.com/prayagsinojia-ops/HR-Attrition-Analysis/blob/main/HR%20d%202.png) 

 Key Insights

- 144 employees identified as High Risk.

- Low Job Satisfaction (Level 2) drives highest attrition (22%).

- Manager rating impacts retention (~22% attrition at mid ratings).

- Overtime is not a major attrition driver.<br>



## 🧠 7. Business Recommendations

Based on analysis:

1️⃣ Improve job satisfaction programs
2️⃣ Leadership development training
3️⃣ Salary review for lower bands
4️⃣ Retention plans for 10+ year employees
5️⃣ Quarterly engagement surveys
6️⃣ Proactive monitoring of high-risk employees
